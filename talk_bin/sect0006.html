4.2 Representations of Hopf algebras

First of all, what a representation of an algebra? Just as group representations are group homomorphisms into the group GL(V)GL(V)GL(V), an algebra representation is an algebra homomorphism:

Definition 4.7

A representation of algebra AAA on vector space VVV is an algebra homomorphism ρ:A→End(V)\rho :A\xrightarrow {}\text{End}(V)ρ:A​End(V), i.e. is a linear map that is also a ring homomorphism: ρ(1)=1\rho (1)=1ρ(1)=1 and ρ(ab)=ρ(a)ρ(b)\rho (ab)=\rho (a)\rho (b)ρ(ab)=ρ(a)ρ(b). Recall End(V)\text{End}(V)End(V) is the endomorphism ring, i.e. the ring of linear maps with addition given pointwise and multiplication given by composition.

Proposition 4.8

The representation category Rep(H)\text{Rep}(H)Rep(H), or equivalently the module category HHH-Mod\text{Mod}Mod, of Hopf algebra HHH is a rigid monoidal category.
